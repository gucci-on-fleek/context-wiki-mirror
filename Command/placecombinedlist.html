Summary

The command \placecombinedlist typesets combined lists

Settings

\placecombinedlist[...][...=...,...]
[...] list
...=...,... inherits from \setupcombinedlist

Description

Typeset a combined list defined with \definecombinedlist . There is one such list predefined: content .

This command does not insert a sectioning head.

Examples

Example 1

General TOC: 

\placecombinedlist[content][criterium=all]

Local TOC: 

\placecombinedlist[content][criterium=local]:

\chapter{Test}
\placecombinedlist[content][criterium=local]
\section{A}
\section{B}

Otherwise; 

\chapter{Test}
\section{Table of contents}
\placecombinedlist[content][criterium=previous]
In this case the contents of the chapter is given.

Although you are already in a lower level in the hierarchy of the document.
\section{B}
